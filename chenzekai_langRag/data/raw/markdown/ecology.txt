Ecosystem dynamics describe how energy and materials move through living systems.
A forest, for example, cycles carbon between trees, soil, and the atmosphere.
Biodiversity often increases resilience because multiple species can fill similar roles.
Human activity changes habitats through land use, pollution, and climate effects.
Conservation strategies focus on protecting core habitats and connecting fragmented areas.

River basins show how upstream actions affect downstream communities.
When vegetation is removed, erosion increases and water quality declines.
Restoring wetlands can slow floods and improve natural filtration.
